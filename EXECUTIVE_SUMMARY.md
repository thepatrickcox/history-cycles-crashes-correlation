# Executive Summary

**What this is.** A cross-axis historical pattern study of US market cycles, political power, and economic stress, 1865–2026, with UK/Germany/Japan comparison. Built by one researcher from primary sources over roughly a decade, consolidated and validated in 2026.

**The distinguishing contribution.** Integration across dimensions usually studied in isolation — market cycles, partisan dynamics, political violence, asset-regulation history, and human-rights inflection points — in a single analytic frame. The cross-axis combination does not appear in any single source in the existing literature, though each underlying axis has its own scholarship.

**The four strongest findings.**

1. **Partisan crash asymmetry** — 11 of 14 major US crashes (1907–2022) under one party; exceeds the base-rate-adjusted expectation. Independently corroborated by Bartels (*Unequal Democracy*) and Blinder & Watson (2016), which the author reached independently.
2. **Assassination–stress clustering** — 10 of 13 documented attempts (1865–2024) fell within two years of a major crash, ~3.5× the random rate. Small sample; presented as observation, not statistical certainty.
3. **Restriction→adoption arc** — silver (1873–1893), gold (1933–1975), crypto (2017–) each followed regulatory restriction then institutional re-engagement. Crypto leg still in progress.
4. **Inter-crash interval shortening** — intervals have shortened alongside the 1971 Bretton Woods end, 1999 Glass-Steagall repeal, and 2018 Dodd-Frank rollback.

**Validation status (May 2026).** The October 2025 crisis forecast is graded PARTIALLY CONFIRMED: the window and tariff trigger were correct; the depth (a real −19% correction) fell short of the −30/−40% historical reference, and recovery was faster than comparable cases. Recorded honestly, miss included.

**Methodological posture.** Counter-examples are retained, not hidden. Sample sizes are stated as small. Claims are restricted to pattern observation; intent claims are excluded as requiring a class of evidence (documents, testimony, improbability) the data do not contain. Valuation figures are being standardized to Shiller CAPE; classification criteria are being pre-registered.

**What this is not.** Not investment advice; describes no positions. Not a claim that any actor arranges market events — only that stated, falsifiable patterns did or did not occur.
