## 2026-07-07 — human-rights timing patterns (tested same day)
7. **Rights events near elections / crashes (general version).** 26 US-era anchored events from the human-rights sheets. Elections: 23/26 within ±1 yr — but 75% of ALL years are within ±1 yr of a presidential election (p = 0.08); on-year 8/26 vs 25% expected (p = 0.32). Crashes: 16/26 within ±2 yr of crash periods vs 45.9% coverage (p = 0.081). Decade-density correlation r = 0.13, permutation p = 0.29 — the two modern bursts (1963–69, 2013–23) co-occur with stress eras, but no general law holds across the record. **Status: weak as stated; dominant caveat is the curated event list — gate on validating against an external, pre-existing rights chronology before retest.**
8. **Rights *setbacks* track economic stress (sharp version).** Of four setback events in the list (Plessy 1896, Prop 8 2008, Dobbs 2022, 2023 legislative wave), the two modern ones land ON crash years and three of four sit in crash-era windows. n = 4 — a note, not a claim — but tighter, mechanistic (scarcity → retrenchment has an academic literature), and falsifiable once events are typed advance/setback under pre-committed definitions. **Status: promising; untested pending typed, externally validated event list.**
