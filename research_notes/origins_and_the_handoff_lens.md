# Origins — The Handoff Lens
*Restored July 2026. This is the founding question of the entire archive, written down properly after years scattered across working sheets. It is also the pre-registration path for the held-back scapegoat/hero columns.*

## The original observation

Markets, debts, and economic conditions do not reset at inaugurations. What one administration hands the next enters the historical record under the successor's dates: the structure loaded through the 1920s is filed under Hoover's name; the credit machinery built across decades is filed under Bush's 2008. History's ledger assigns by calendar. Blame assigns by occupancy. Those are two different bookkeeping systems, and the gap between them is measurable.

This was the first question the project ever asked — who inherited what, who handed what forward — and every later axis (crashes by party, severity classes, the per-administration spine) grew from it.

## The two testable claims it yields

**1. The inheritance ledger.** For each presidential handoff, record the measured conditions at inauguration: market valuation (CAPE), inflation, policy and long rates, debt/GDP, unemployment. Neutral numbers, public sources, no judgment column. The ledger makes "what Biden handed Trump" and "what Trump hands next" the same kind of fact as a crash date — and it converts every future argument about blame into a row lookup.

**2. The lightning-rod thesis.** Crash-adjacent presidents absorb reputational blame disproportionate to their measured inheritance. Testable: historian ranking movements and contemporary coverage versus the ledger's inherited-conditions row. Type case: Hoover, who carries 1929 in full while the ledger shows the structure loaded before his seventh month. No intent required — blame flows to the most visible office because that is how narrative works. This is the defensible, pre-registered form of the original "scapegoat" column: *conditions inherited* (measured) versus *blame assigned* (measured), with the motive question permanently out of scope.

## What the timing tests did to the founding hunch (July 2026)

The original hunch included timing: crashes serving electoral advantage should cluster at midterms or election years ("max pain"). Tested against the workbook's own fifteen events (`analysis/statistical_tests.py`): crash starts distribute **5 / 4 / 3 / 3** across term years one through four — statistically uniform (year-1 lean raw p = 0.31; corrected for post-hoc bin selection p = 0.94). Election years hold 3 of 15, *below* uniform expectation. Start-month seasonality tested equally weak.

Stated plainly: **the *when* shows no structure. The signal in this archive lives entirely in the *who* (12–3 by party) and the *how severe* (all nine full crash-terms under one party).** That pattern is what structural explanations predict — policy regimes and deregulation cycles loading the machine — and is the opposite of what timed construction would produce. An orchestrator would time; incentives don't need to. The founding hunch was half wrong, the archive's own machine caught it, and what survived is stronger and points away from the domino-tipper the hunch once suspected.

## A related axis, defined for future build: impeachment pressure

Formal impeachment and impeachment *pressure* are different datasets, and the definitional rule must be pre-committed before any row is entered, because the tiers differ enormously in meaning:
- Tier 1: impeached by House vote (Johnson 1868; Clinton 1998; Trump 2019, 2021)
- Tier 2: articles reported by committee, no floor vote (Nixon 1974 — resigned before the vote; never impeached)
- Tier 3: impeachment resolution introduced (numerous, both parties, most administrations since Tyler 1843)
- Tier 4: formal inquiry opened
Motivating observation, logged without tallies: several high-pressure episodes sit near market-stress eras (1868/1869, 1932–33, 1974, 1987, 2019–21). Whether that survives a real base rate is exactly what the column, built blind under the tier rule, would test. No party counts are recorded here; the data will show what it shows.

*Pattern, never intent. The ledger measures handoffs; it does not read minds.*
