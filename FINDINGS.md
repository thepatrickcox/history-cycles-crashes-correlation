# History, Cycles, and Crashes

### A Cross-Axis Study of US Market Crashes, Political Power, Economic Stress, Asset Suppression, and Human Rights Inflection Points — 1865 to 2026

**By Patrick Cox**  
*Independent researcher. Dataset assembled ~2015–2026 from primary sources.*

---

## The Question

Around 2015 I started asking a simple question:

*Do US market crashes follow a pattern — and if so, what travels with them?*

Not whether crashes happen. Everyone knows they happen. The question was whether the conditions surrounding them — who holds political power, what assets are being regulated, what's happening to ordinary people's economic stress — recur in identifiable structural ways across time.

I started with a spreadsheet. A president, a crash year, a percentage decline, a recovery time. That was it.

Over the next decade, working from primary sources — government data, contemporaneous press, Federal Reserve records, Congressional Research Service documents, NBER data — the question expanded. The spreadsheet became twenty-eight spreadsheets. The twenty-eight became a normalized database of 87 observations spanning 1865 to 2026, covering market events, political violence, asset regulation cycles, human rights inflection points, and forward-looking indicators across the United States, United Kingdom, Germany, and Japan.

I tested things that didn't hold. Hurricane frequency mapped against crash timing — evenly distributed, discarded. Specific family wealth claims circulating in financial commentary — insufficient verifiable evidence, moved to a clearly labeled speculative archive. Anything that required intent to be true rather than pattern recurrence was separated out.

What remained after that process is what this repository contains.

## What I Did Not Know When I Built This

I built this framework without prior knowledge of the academic literature in this space.

I did not know that Larry Bartels (Vanderbilt) had documented partisan asymmetry in economic outcomes in *Unequal Democracy* (2008). I did not know that Alan Blinder and Mark Watson had quantified the GDP growth differential between Democratic and Republican administrations in the *American Economic Review* (2016). I did not know that Carmen Reinhart and Kenneth Rogoff had mapped debt crisis patterns across eight centuries in *This Time Is Different* (2009).

I ordered those books in May 2026. They are currently unread, sitting in my to-read pile.

I am documenting this not as a boast but as a methodological fact. When two researchers working independently from different starting points arrive at overlapping conclusions, that convergence is itself evidence that something real is being observed. It is not proof — but it is meaningful.

Where my findings overlap with established academic literature, I say so explicitly and treat the academic work as corroboration, not as the origin of the observation. Where my findings have no established parallel that I can identify, I say that too.

## The Findings

### Finding 1 — Partisan Asymmetry in Major US Crash Incidence
*Original. Independently corroborated by peer-reviewed literature the author had not encountered.*

Of fifteen documented major US market events from 1907 through 2025, twelve occurred during Republican administrations and three during Democratic administrations.

Republican presidents held office for approximately 64 of those years versus 51 for Democrats, so a base-rate-adjusted expectation produces roughly 9R/5D. The observed 12R/3D ratio exceeds that expectation. The sample size precludes statistical certainty — this is a pattern observation and falsifiable hypothesis, not a proven law.

**What would falsify it:** Three or more consecutive Democratic-led crashes, or a 30-year period in which the partisan ratio inverts.

**Academic parallel identified afterward:** Bartels (2008) and Blinder-Watson (2016) documented parallel asymmetries in economic growth and income outcomes under Democratic versus Republican administrations using econometric methods. Their framing differs from mine; the convergence is what matters.

**Current status (May 2026):** The 2025 tariff-induced correction under Trump (Republican, second term) extends the pattern. Ratio now 12R/3D across 15 events.

### Finding 2 — Assassination Attempt Clustering Near Economic Stress
*Original. No established academic parallel identified.*

Of thirteen documented assassination attempts on US presidents, major candidates, and former presidents from 1865 to 2024, ten occurred within two years of a major market crash event.

If attempts were uniformly distributed across the 160-year span, approximately 22% would fall within a 2-year crash window by chance. The observed rate is approximately 77% — roughly 3.5 times the random base rate.

Notable clustering events:
- **Ford 1975:** Two attempts within 17 days, immediately following the 1973–74 oil crisis bear market
- **Trump 2024:** Two attempts within nine weeks, immediately preceding the projected 2025 stress window

**What would falsify it:** A 25-year period of major economic stability accompanied by a continuing rate of attempts.

**Academic parallel identified afterward:** A literature on economic distress and aggregate political violence exists (Walter; Pinker). The specific assassination-attempt clustering documented here appears to be an original observation.

**Current status (May 2026):** Pattern confirmed. The 2024 Trump double-attempt preceded the 2025 tariff correction. No new attempts 2025–2026 to date.

### Finding 3 — Asset Suppression Arc: Silver → Gold → Bitcoin
*Original framing. No single academic work treats these three as a unified recurring template.*

Three major non-sovereign stores of value have followed a structurally identical pattern across 150 years:

**Phase 1 — Popular adoption** disrupts incumbent monetary arrangements.  
**Phase 2 — Regulatory restriction** targets the most legally vulnerable point — the corporate intermediary when one exists, the asset directly when it does not.  
**Phase 3 — Price suppression** follows restriction, often severe.  
**Phase 4 — Institutional adoption** follows on terms favorable to incumbent financial actors. The asset survives at a higher inflation-adjusted price than before restriction began.

| Asset | Restriction Event | Immediate Decline | Post-Restriction Rebound |
|---|---|---|---|
| Silver | Sherman Act repeal 1893 | -25% | Demonetized; eventually institutionalized |
| Gold | EO 6102 confiscation 1933 | Forced at $20.67 | +678% (inflation-adjusted) after 1975 restoration |
| Bitcoin | China ICO ban 2017; mining ban 2021; US regulatory pressure 2020–2024 | -17% to -41% per event | +400% post-China ban; ETF approval Jan 2024 |

**A structural note on the Bitcoin regulatory approach:** Bitcoin has no CEO, no headquarters, no legal entity. It cannot be sued. When US regulators sought a legal handle on the crypto asset class, they targeted Ripple Labs — a company — rather than Bitcoin directly. This is structurally identical to how 1890s regulators targeted silver mining *companies* rather than silver itself. The asset is too distributed to attack directly; the corporate intermediary is the accessible point. This is regulatory behavior following the path of least legal resistance, documented across three asset classes and 150 years.

**What would falsify it:** Crypto adoption proceeding without institutional capture; a fourth major non-sovereign asset class failing to follow the pattern.

**Current status (May 2026):** Phase 4 institutional adoption confirmed — Bitcoin ETF assets ~$100B+, corporate treasury adoption active, nation-state reserves emerging. BTC ~$77–82K (May 2026), down from $106K peak (Nov 2025).

### Finding 4 — Inter-Crash Interval Shortening Since Deregulation
*Original observation. Corroborated directionally by established academic literature.*

The interval between major US crash events from 1929 to 1970 was 41 years — the longest in the dataset. Subsequent intervals have compressed:

| Interval | Years |
|---|---|
| 1929 → 1970 | 41 |
| 1970 → 1973 | 3 |
| 1973 → 1987 | 14 |
| 1987 → 2000 | 13 |
| 2000 → 2008 | 8 |
| 2008 → 2020 | 12 |
| 2020 → 2022 | 2 |
| 2022 → 2025 | 3 |

Major regulatory rollback events bookend the compression: end of Bretton Woods (1971), repeal of Glass-Steagall (1999), partial Dodd-Frank rollback (2018).

**What would falsify it:** A 30-year crash-free period following a major reregulation event.

**Academic parallel identified afterward:** Reinhart and Rogoff (*This Time Is Different*) and Roubini and Mihm (*Crisis Economics*) document the relationship between financial deregulation and crisis frequency. This finding is a directionally correct independent version of a well-established academic observation.

### Finding 5 (New, 2026) — Gold as 12–24 Month Leading Crisis Indicator
*Original. No established parallel identified.*

Gold peaked at $5,600/oz in January 2026. Current price: ~$4,523 (May 2026). Year-over-year gain: +40%.

The original framework forecast gold above $3,500 as a 2028 condition. It arrived in 2026 — approximately 18–24 months early. If the 12–24 month lead relationship holds, the January 2026 peak points toward a watch window of January–June 2027.

This finding is new and has one data point. It is documented here as an emerging observation, not a confirmed pattern. The falsification criterion is straightforward: gold retreating below $2,500 for six or more consecutive months without a corresponding crisis would substantially weaken it.

## What I Tested and Discarded

The dataset now contains 87 observations and 11 findings. It previously contained more of both.

**Hurricane frequency** was mapped against crash timing. Distribution was statistically even. Discarded.

**Named family wealth figures** circulating in financial commentary (specific asset-controlled estimates for major banking dynasties) were investigated. The figures are unverifiable from public accounting. Removed from the primary dataset, moved to a clearly labeled speculative archive that is not part of this public repository.

**Intent and orchestration claims** — the hypothesis that named actors coordinate crash timing deliberately — were tracked throughout the research. The data support pattern observation. They do not support the further inference that patterns are deliberately engineered. These claims require a different class of evidence: documents, communications, testimony, or coincidence so improbable as to rule out chance. That evidence is not present. These hypotheses remain in a private speculative archive.

Showing what didn't make the cut is as important as showing what did. A framework that only confirms is not a framework — it is a bias.

## 2025 Forecast Validation — Honest Accounting

In October 2025, this framework identified time windows worth watching based on historical timing. Below is the honest record.

| Forecast | Original (Oct 2025) | Actual (May 2026) | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2025 crisis probability | 45% | S&P -19% (Feb 19–Apr 8). Did not reach -30 to -40% reference depth. Market +17% full year. | **Partially confirmed.** Timeframe correct (year 1, Q1-Q2). Trigger correct (tariffs). Depth missed — actual was half of historical average reference. |
| 2025 tariff trigger | Flagged as primary risk | Effective tariff rate ~24% enacted | **Confirmed** |
| Consumer delinquency >4% | Forecast as 2027 condition | 4.8% reached Q4 2025 — 12–18 months early | **Early trigger** |
| Gold >$3,500 | Forecast as 2028 signal | Reached Jan 2026, peaked $5,600 | **Pre-fired — 2 years early** |
| 2026 window | 35% crisis probability | Active. S&P ~7,473 (+8.5% YTD May 2026). Preconditions intensifying. | **In window** |

The -30 to -40% figure was a historical average reference point, not a committed prediction. The 2025 correction was real, the timeframe was right, and the depth was shallower than historical averages. That is logged here as a miss on magnitude, a hit on timing and trigger.

A researcher willing to mark predictions wrong gains standing that decades of correct predictions cannot purchase.

**Current watch windows:**
- November 2026 midterm elections
- Q1 2027 (corporate debt refinancing wall; student loan charge-offs; gold lead indicator timing)

## What This Is Not

This is not investment advice.

This is not proof of orchestration by any actor, named or unnamed.

This is not a finished academic paper. The methodology is observational. The sample sizes for several findings are small. The correct framing is: these are historical patterns and falsifiable hypotheses, documented honestly, that warrant attention from anyone thinking seriously about market cycles.

This is a decade of independent work from primary sources, offered openly for others to examine, extend, challenge, or refute.
